What Is Accounting?

🧾

Simple Meaning

Accounting is the system used to record, classify, summarize, and report business transactions.

👀

Why People Use It

It helps owners, investors, and managers understand profit, cash, assets, and obligations.

Main Accounting Elements

Assets

Things the business owns, like cash and equipment.

Liabilities

Amounts the business owes to others.

Equity

Owner's claim after liabilities are deducted from assets.

Revenue

Income earned from providing goods or services.

Expenses

Costs paid or used to earn revenue.

Profit

The excess of revenue over expenses.

The Accounting Equation

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

This is the foundation of financial accounting and explains why the balance sheet must always balance.

Assets ↑

Cash, receivables, inventory.

Liabilities ↑

Loans, payables, wages payable.

Equity ↑

Owner capital and retained earnings.

The Four Main Statements

Balance Sheet

Shows assets, liabilities, and equity at a certain date.

Income Statement

Shows revenue, expenses, and profit for a period.

Cash Flow Statement

Explains how cash moved in and out of the business.

Statement of Equity

Shows changes in owner or shareholder equity.

What Is a Journal Entry?

✍️

Definition

A journal entry is the first formal record of a business transaction in the accounting system.

📅

What It Includes

Date, affected accounts, debit amount, credit amount, and a short explanation.

Debit and Credit Rules

Account TypeIncrease WithDecrease With
AssetsDebitCredit
ExpensesDebitCredit
LiabilitiesCreditDebit
EquityCreditDebit
RevenueCreditDebit

How to Write a Journal Entry

3

4

Balance

Debits must equal credits

Transaction: The owner invests $10,000 cash into the business.

Transaction: The company buys equipment for $3,000 cash.

Cash$3,000

Transaction: The business pays $800 for monthly rent.

AccountDebitCredit

Cash$800

Example 5: Buy Supplies on Credit

Transaction: The company buys supplies for $500 on account.

AccountDebitCredit

Common Beginner Mistakes

Confusing debit with increase

Debit does not always mean increase; it depends on the account type.

Forgetting balance

Every journal entry must have total debits equal total credits.

Choosing wrong account

Use the exact account affected, not a vague description.

Ignoring the explanation

A short note makes the entry easier to review later.

Now you know the basics

Accounting starts with understanding accounts, the accounting equation, and financial statements. Journal entries then record each transaction using equal debits and credits.
